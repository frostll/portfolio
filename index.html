About Me

I am a master's student at the University of Michigan School of Information, specializing in Library Science and Archives. Before beginning my current course of study, I completed a doctorate in English literature at Saint Louis University. During my years as a doctoral student, I served an internship at the Center for Medieval and Renaissance Studies, in which I worked in both Rare Books, cataloging items from the Moore Carpenter Recusant History Collection, and the Vatican Film Library. I have also published a number of scholarly articles.


I also work as a freelance copy editor and proofreader and have done work for Princeton University Press. If you need a proofreader for your thesis or manuscript, feel free to contact me! I'm particularly interested in/qualified to comment on medieval and early modern European subjects, but will proofread anything. I'm less interested in editing fiction, though. Languages I read other than English (for the purposes of proofreading English-language texts dealing with quotations in other languages) include French, Latin, and Old and Middle English; I also have some rudimentary German and Czech.


In my spare time, I enjoy writing, theater, traveling, and music. I play piano, guitar, mandolin, tinwhistle, bowed psaltery, and autoharp (albeit none of them all that well) and have been singing in choirs for nearly thirty years. Before the COVID-19 pandemic I sang with the MSU Choral Union and hope to return when group singing is safe again.
